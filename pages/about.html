01Origin

Born from
the battlefield.

Muay Thai grew out of Muay Boran, the unarmed combat used by Siamese soldiers when their weapons broke on the battlefield. Villages held matches in dirt pits with rope-wrapped fists. Kings fought. Monks trained. By the 1920s the sport moved into modern rings, adopted timed rounds and boxing gloves but kept its soul: the ritual, the music, the elbows.

Today it is the national sport of Thailand, practiced in over 100 countries, and the striking foundation for most of modern MMA.
